Major Sam Manekshaw

Sam Hormusji Framji Jamshedji Manekshaw

Chief of the Army Staff of the Indian Army during the Indo-Pakistani War of 1971 and the first Indian Army officer to be promoted to the rank of field marshal.

Here's a time line of Sam Manekshaw

  • 1914 - Born in Amritsar, Punjab, British India
  • 1932 - Graduated from Sherwood College, Nainital
  • 1934 - Attended the Indian Military Academy, Dehradun
  • 1935 - Commissioned as a Second Lieutenant in the British Indian Army
  • 1942 - Fought in World War II in the Burma Campaign, severely wounded
  • 1947 - Promoted to the rank of Lieutenant Colonel and served in the Indo-Pakistani War of 1947-1948
  • 1961 - Promoted to the rank of Major General
  • 1963 - Became the Commandant of the Defence Services Staff College, Wellington
  • 1964 - Promoted to the rank of Lieutenant General and took charge of the Eastern Command
  • 1969 - Became the Chief of the Army Staff of the Indian Army
  • 1971 - Led the Indian Army to victory in the Indo-Pakistani War of 1971, leading to the creation of Bangladesh
  • 1972 - Awarded the Padma Vibhushan, India's second-highest civilian award
  • 1973 - Promoted to the rank of Field Marshal, the first Indian Army officer to be conferred the rank
  • 2008 - Passed away at the age of 94 in Wellington, Tamil Nadu, India

"Sam was an outstanding leader of men and a fine military leader. He had the rare knack of leading from the front and keeping his cool under difficult circumstances."

-- Air Chief Marshal P.C. Lal

If you have time, you should read more about this incredible human being on his Wikipedia entry.
